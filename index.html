Project Narrative — STAR Method
S
Situation

A company runs the same campaigns on two platforms — Facebook and Google AdWords — under a fixed, limited marketing budget. Leadership had no reliable way to tell which platform was actually delivering better returns; decisions were made on raw averages and channel familiarity, not evidence.

Project Narrative — STAR Method
T
Task

Analyze 1,000 daily campaign records across both platforms, design metrics that reflect true return on spend, and determine whether the performance gap between platforms was statistically meaningful — then turn that into a budget recommendation leadership could act on.

Project Narrative — STAR Method
A
Action
  • Cleaned and validated the dataset — type casting, null/duplicate checks, date parsing
  • Engineered a cost-per-conversion metric to unify performance and spend into one number
  • Bucketed conversions on a shared scale so platforms were judged consistently
  • Tested normality and variance before choosing between a t-test or Mann-Whitney U — rather than defaulting to one method
Project Narrative — STAR Method
R
Result

Facebook converts at ~3x the rate of AdWords, at a 37% lower cost per conversion. AdWords delivers ~2x the reach at a lower cost-per-click. All five metrics tested were statistically significant — turning an opinion-based debate into a defensible, evidence-backed recommendation.

Business Problem

Every dollar spent on one platform is a dollar not spent on the other

Without statistical validation, teams risk shifting budget toward metrics that look different but aren't reliably different — or missing real differences hidden in daily noise.

"Does platform choice make a measurable, statistically defensible difference — and if so, which platform earns more budget for which objective?"
Data & Methodology

Five steps, each one gating the next

Dataset

1,000 daily records (2021–2023), Facebook & AdWords tracked in parallel — views, clicks, conversions, CTR, CPC, cost-per-ad. No missing values or duplicates.

  1. Clean & validate — types, nulls, dates
  2. Explore — distributions, correlation matrix, trends
  3. Engineer metrics — cost-per-conversion
  4. Bucket fairly — shared conversion bands
  5. Test rigorously — normality → variance → correct test

Recommendation

Verdict

Prioritize Facebook for conversion-focused campaigns — higher CTR and conversion efficiency lower cost per result. Use AdWords for low-cost reach and awareness, where its cheaper clicks and larger footprint matter most. A blended strategy captures each platform's real strength.
